Enola Holmes
On her sixteenth birthday, Enola wakes to find that her mother has disappeared, leaving behind only some gifts. A week later, she meets her brothers, Mycroft and Sherlock. Sherlock finds her intelligent, whereas Mycroft finds her troublesome and as her legal guardian, intends to send her away to a finishing school run by Miss Harrison. 		Director:
Harry Bradbeer
Stars:
Millie Bobby Brown, Louis Partridge, Helena Bonham Carter
Hilmii Yusuf Shahril:
a movie that screams feminism . i absolutely love this movie !! millie bobby brown played the role of enola fantastically . enola is shown as a rebel of her time who doesn't conform to the standard of what women are supposed to be like . brave , confident and beautiful , enola stops at nothing to find her "missing" mother and meets new people along the way . i love how she talks to the audience to keep it engaging and exciting . the amount of suspense keeps your heart pumping throughout the movie . henry and sam played the roles of her older brothers very well and it was nice to see a sense of humour put into all their roles . this movie sends the message that women are very capable on their own and do not need to depend on a man to solve their problems . neat cinematography , good story line , this movie gets nothing less than around 8-9/10.can't wait for Enola Holmes 2 to be out in 2022, which is in abt a month's time now !!

I honestly loved this movie. I found it so interesting and psychological the whole way through with time loops, endless questions and just such creative visual choices. Yes there were some plot holes and a whole lot of questions left unanswered however, I feel that the fact these questions are left unanswered adds to the movie.
